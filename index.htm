Short Summary


I have recently completed my Ph.D. in Computational Social Science at ETH Zurich. My main research interests are:
  • incentives schemes for peer review systems,
  • consensus formation and social influence, in particular in epistemic communities,
  • equality and efficiency in public-goods games,
  • philosophy of science, in particular Paul Feyerabend's body of work.
 My methodology of investigation aims at bringing together agent-based computer simulations and laboratory and online behavioral experiments.

I am also an active developer. I created a JavaScript platform for conducting real-time online behavioral experiments directly in the browser called nodeGame (http://www.nodegame.org). I have been also project leader of the QScience platform for the QLectives project (http://www.qlectives.eu).

From January 2016 I will be joining Prof. Lazer's research group (http://www.lazerlab.net) at the Network Science Institute at NorthEastern Univesity, Boston (MA).
